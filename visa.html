Common Visa Officer Questions

Know what to say — and what NOT to say

✗ Wrong Answer

"Because it's free education and I love cars."


✓ Right Approach

Focus on the quality of education, research facilities, and the specific curriculum of your course. Mention that German degrees are practice-oriented and globally respected.

"Germany is a global leader in [Your Field]. The curriculum at [Your University] offers a module on [Specific Subject] which directly aligns with my career goals in [Area]. The combination of academic rigor and industry integration in Germany is unmatched."
✗ Never say

"Because I got admission here."


✓ Show you researched it
  • Mention a specific professor's research work
  • Mention a specific lab or research center at that university
  • Name the course modules that excite you
  • Mention the university's ranking in your field
✗ Avoid

"I want to settle in Germany forever." — This can imply permanent immigration intent, which visa officers are cautious about.


✓ Balanced Answer
"My goal is to become an expert in [Field]. After my Masters, I plan to gain 1–2 years of international work experience in the German industry to apply my learnings, and eventually contribute back to my field globally."

Be very clear and confident about your finances. The visa officer wants to confirm you won't run out of money.

"My [father/parents] are sponsoring me. [He/She] is a [Job Title] with an annual income of [Amount]. We also have a blocked account with €11,208 deposited at [Coracle / Expatrio / Deutsche Bank], which covers my first year's living expenses."

Be honest, but show initiative.

"My program is taught in English, so I don't need German for my studies. However, I have started learning German basics and I'm excited to improve my language skills once I'm in Germany, as I understand it will help me integrate better."

This is a key differentiator question. Show you actively chose Germany, not just as a fallback.

  • Germany's engineering & technical education is world-class
  • Close collaboration between universities and leading industries (Bosch, Siemens, SAP, etc.)
  • Affordable education compared to the US/UK
  • Structured career pathways after graduation

Stay calm and show you have a backup plan. Don't say you'll give up.

"I would immediately find out the reason for rejection and address whatever was missing. I am very committed to this program and would reapply with the corrected documentation."
💡 Pro Tips from Someone Who's Been Through It:

Dress professionally — treat it like a job interview, not a casual appointment.
Be confident and concise — answer what's asked, don't over-explain.
Know your SoP inside out — the VO may ask you to elaborate on anything you wrote.
Carry everything in an organized folder — fumbling for documents creates a bad impression.
Arrive 15 minutes early — rushing in stressed is never a good start.
